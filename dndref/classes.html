Barbarian

Class Information

Hit Points
Hit Dice: 1d12 per Level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 12 + Con modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d12 (or 7) + your Con modifier per Barbarian level after 1st


Proficiencies
Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution
Skills: Choose two from: Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival


Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
  • A greataxe OR any martial melee weapon
  • Two handaxes OR any simple weapon
  • An explorer's pack
  • Four javelins
Alternatively, you may start with 2d4×10 gp and purchase your starting equipment using these funds.

Class Features

Rage
You may enter a Rage as a bonus action.
While raging, you gain the following benefits if you aren't wearing heavy armor:
  • Advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws
  • Gain a bonus to melee weapon damage as shown on the advancement table
  • Resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage
Your rage lasts 1 minute. It ends early if:
  • You are knocked unconscious
  • Your turn ends and you haven't dealt or received damage
  • You choose to end your rage as a bonus action
Your uses of Rage replenish after a long rest.


Unarmored Defense
While you are not wearing any armor, your Armor Class equals 10 + Dex mod + Con mod. You may use a shield and retain this effect.


Reckless Attack
On your first attack of your turn, you may opt to gain advantage on an attack; however, attacks made against you also gain advantage until your next turn.


Danger Sense
You gain advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects you can't see, such as traps or spells. You may not gain this effect ifyou are blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.


Primal Path
Choose a path to shape the nature of your rage: Path of the Berzerker, or Path of the Totem Warrior. (pp. 49-50 PHB)


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Extra Attack
You may attack twice whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.


Fast Movement
Your speed increases by 10 feet when you aren't wearing heavy armor.


Feral Instinct
Gain advantage on Initiative rolls. Additionally, if you are Surprised (but not incapacitated) at the beginning of combat, you may act normally on your first turn if you enter your Rage as your first action.


Brutal Critical
Roll one additional weapon damage die when determining critical damage on a melee attack. This increases to two dice at 13th level, and three dice at 17th level.


Relentless Rage
If you drop to 0 hit points while raging, and don't die outright, you may make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. On success, drop to 1 hit point instead. Each time you use this feature (after the first), the DC increases by 5. It resets to DC 10 after a long rest.


Persistent Rage
Your rage only ends early if you choose to end it or fall unconscious.


Indomitable Might
If your total for a Strength check is less than your Strength score, you may use your score in place of the check.


Primal Champion
Your Strength and Constitution scores increase by 4. Your maximum for those scores is now 24.

Class Features

Spellcasting
Your spellcasting ability is Charisma.

Your spell save DC is 8 + Proficiency mod + Char mod, and your spell attack modifier is Proficiency bonus + Char mod.

You may cast any Bard spell as a Ritual, so long as it has the Ritual tag.
You may use any musical instrument as a spellcasting focus for your Bard spells.


Bardic Inspiration
You may use a bonus action to give one creature within 60 feet who can hear you, other than yourself, a bonus die.
Once in the next 10 minutes, that creature may roll that die and add it to any ability check, attack roll, or saving throw.
A creature may only have one Bardic Inspiration die at a time.
You may use this feature a number of times equal to your Char mod, and expended uses are refilled after a long rest.


Jack of All Trades
You may add half your proficiency bonus, rounded down, to any ability check you make that doesn't already include your proficiency bonus.


Song of Rest
During a short rest, you may play a soothing song. If you, or any friendly creatures, regain hit points during the rest, they regain an extra die (based on your level).


Bard College
Delve into the advanced techniques of a bard college: the College of Lore or the College of Valor. (pp. 54-55 PHB)


Expertise
Choose two skill proficiencies; your proficiency bonus is doubled for any Ability check that uses either of those proficiencies.


Ability Score Improvement
Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or two ability scores of your choice by 1. You may not increase an ability score beyond 20.


Font of Inspiration
You may now regain expended uses of Bardic Inspiration after a short or long rest.


Countercharm
Take an action, which lasts until the end of your next turn, to grant you and any friendly creatures within 30 feet (who can hear you) Advantage on saving throws against being Frightened or Charmed. This ends early if you are incapacitated or silenced, or if you voluntarily end it.


Magical Secrets
Choose two spells from any class, including this one. They must be of a level you can cast, or a cantrip. The chosen spells count as Bard spells (cast using Charisma) and are included in the number of Spells Known in the advancement table.


Superior Inspiration
If you have no uses of Bardic Inspiration left when you roll initiative, regain one.

Wizard

Class Information

 
Hit Points
Hit Dice: 1d6 per Level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + Con modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Con modifier per Wizard level after 1st


Proficiencies
Armor: None
Weapons: Daggers, darts, slings, quarterstaffs, light crossbows
Tools: None
Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from: Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Religion

Equipment
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
  • A quarterstaff OR a dagger
  • A component pouch OR an arcane focus
  • A scholar's pack OR an explorer's pack
  • A spellbook
Alternatively, you may start with 4d4×10 gp and purchase your starting equipment using these funds.

Additional information: pp. 112-119 PHB
